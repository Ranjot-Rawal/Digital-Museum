James Webb Space Telescope

Peering deeper into the universe than ever before.

Introduction

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is the world's most powerful space observatory. Developed by NASA in collaboration with the European Space Agency (ESA) and the Canadian Space Agency (CSA), it was launched on December 25, 2021. Designed to observe the universe in infrared light, the telescope allows scientists to study distant galaxies, newborn stars, planets, and cosmic phenomena hidden from ordinary telescopes.

Orbiting approximately 1.5 million kilometers from Earth at the second Sun-Earth Lagrange point (L2), Webb operates in the cold darkness of space, allowing its highly sensitive instruments to detect extremely faint infrared signals from the earliest stages of the universe.

Mission Objectives

  • Study the first galaxies formed after the Big Bang.
  • Observe the birth and evolution of stars.
  • Investigate distant exoplanets and their atmospheres.
  • Understand the formation of galaxies and black holes.
  • Search for conditions that could support life.

Major Achievements

Since becoming operational in 2022, James Webb has captured the deepest infrared images ever recorded, revealed detailed structures inside stellar nurseries, detected water vapor and carbon dioxide in exoplanet atmospheres, and provided astronomers with unprecedented insight into the early universe.
